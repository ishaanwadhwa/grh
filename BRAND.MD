# GOOD ROOM HOUSE

**Product Requirements Document (PRD)**

A boutique, experience-first, design-forward micro resort & community-led hospitality brand

---

## 1. Overview

**Product Name:** GOOD ROOM HOUSE

**Category:** Boutique Micro Resort + Restaurants + Cultural Community Platform

**Location (Phase 1):** Jaipur, India

### Core Idea

GOOD ROOM HOUSE is a **hospitality brand** — not a single property. It is the parent identity behind a growing network of micro resorts, restaurants, and community spaces. Each location is an expression of the brand, rooted in local context but united by a shared philosophy.

The brand is a curated social universe for people with taste. It blends hospitality, design, food, and community into one cohesive, emotionally resonant experience.

Each property exists at the intersection of:
- A micro resort
- A members club (without feeling exclusionary)
- A cultural playground
- A design-forward social space

**Jaipur is the first location (Phase 1), not the entirety of the brand.**

---

## 2. Vision & Philosophy

### Vision

To redefine luxury hospitality in India by making it:
- More personal
- More design-forward
- More community-driven
- Less performative, more felt

### Philosophy

Luxury is no longer about marble and excess. It is about taste, intimacy, and energy.

**GOOD ROOM HOUSE rejects:**
- Generic luxury hotels
- Soulless Airbnbs
- Overly formal fine dining

**Instead, it builds:**
- Spaces that feel like someone interesting lives here
- Experiences that feel unplanned but intentional
- A vibe that attracts the right people naturally

---

## 3. Target Audience

### Primary Audience
- Creatives, founders, designers, cultural thinkers
- Aged ~23–60
- Urban (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, global travellers)
- High taste, not necessarily ultra-rich
- Social but selective

### Psychographics

They:
- Care about aesthetics and energy
- Prefer "who is there" over "what is offered"
- Are tired of mainstream luxury and overhyped places
- Want to feel like insiders, not customers

---

## 4. Core Product Components

### 4.1 Micro Resort (Stay Experience)

**Format:**
- Intimate property (few rooms, scale-driven)
- Each room = distinct personality

**Key Features:**
- Design-first interiors (story-driven, not generic luxury)
- Emotional comfort > standardised service
- Hosts > staff
- Guests feel like friends of the house

**Experience Goals:**
- "I don't want to leave"
- "I met the most interesting people here"
- "This place has a soul"

### 4.2 Restaurants (2 Launch Venues)

**Positioning:** Not just restaurants — social anchors of the brand

**Core Ideas:**
- Chef-driven but not pretentious
- Menu + music + crowd = one experience
- High aesthetic value (visually viral, but not try-hard)

**Role in Ecosystem:**
- Entry point for a new audience
- Daily cultural touchpoint
- Revenue driver + brand builder

### 4.3 Community Layer

This is the moat.

GOOD ROOM HOUSE is building a soft-membership, high-taste community.

**Mechanics:**
- No formal "membership wall" initially
- Access through:
  - Events (Tiny Desk Concerts, etc.)
  - Dinners
  - Social overlap
  - Digital presence (Instagram as a funnel)

**Outcome:**

People feel:
- "I found my people"
- "I want to come back"
- "I want to bring my people here"

## 6. User Journey

**Step 1: Discovery**
- Instagram Reels (primary growth engine)
- Word-of-mouth
- Social curiosity ("What is this place?")

**Step 2: First Interaction**
- Visits a restaurant / attends an event
- Feels:
  - Visual intrigue
  - Social curiosity
  - Slight exclusivity

**Step 3: Immersion**
- Comes back
- Meets people
- Builds familiarity

**Step 4: Conversion**
- Books stay or becomes regular
- Brings friends

**Step 5: Advocacy**
- Organic promotion
- Social media sharing
- Becomes part of the "tribe"

---

## 7. Experience Design Principles

**Intentional Imperfection**
- Not everything should feel polished or predictable

**Layered Discovery**
- The space reveals itself over time

**Social Serendipity**
- Design interactions without forcing them

**Founder Imprint**
- The brand feels like a person, not a company

---

## 8. Competitive Positioning

### Against Traditional Luxury Hotels
- Less formal
- More personality
- More intimate

### Against Airbnbs
- More curated
- More social
- More reliable energy

### Against Trendy Cafes/Restaurants
- Deeper identity
- Stronger community
- Long-term relationship vs one-time visit

---

## 9. Growth Strategy

**Phase 1 (Jaipur Launch)**
- Build a cult following via Instagram
- Launch 2 restaurants as entry points
- Seed community through events + social

**Phase 2**
- Strengthen the micro resort experience
- Introduce repeat rituals (dinners, gatherings)

**Phase 3**
- Expand to:
  - New locations
  - Collaborations
  - Cultural programming

---

**Primary Platform:** Instagram

**Content Pillars:**

## 11. Success Metrics

**Short-Term**
- Instagram growth & engagement
- Restaurant footfall
- Repeat visitors

**Mid-Term**
- Community density (familiar faces)
- Stay bookings
- Organic word-of-mouth

**Long-Term**
- Brand cult status
- Expansion demand
- Cultural relevance

---

## 12. Non-Goals (Important)

GOOD ROOM HOUSE is NOT trying to:
- Scale like a hotel chain
- Appeal to everyone
- Be overly luxurious in a traditional sense
- Become transactional

---

## 13. Key Risks

- Becoming too mainstream → losing edge
- Over-curation → feeling forced
- Scaling too fast → diluting energy
- The founder is not being visible enough

---
